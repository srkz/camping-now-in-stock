UHF · 3900 W ALAMEDA AVE · DM04

The Tower — BARC’s planned UHF repeater.

Our primary repeater will live on the roof of the Tower building in the Burbank media district — a ~32-story high-rise with an unobstructed view of the San Fernando Valley, the LA basin, and everything in between.

BARC primary repeater (planned)

The club is currently working with the SCRRBA on a UHF (440 MHz band) frequency coordination for the Tower site. We expect to be on the air later this year with a standard +5.000 MHz offset and a CTCSS access tone to manage interference from the many adjacent LA-basin systems.

The Tower’s height — well above the hills that divide the basin from the valley — should give clean handheld-radio coverage from Sun Valley to Glendale and out toward Griffith Park, with mobile coverage reaching into the eastern San Fernando Valley and the Crescenta Valley.

Final parameters will be published here and in the first newsletter once the coordination is issued and the antenna is on the roof.

How to use the repeater

If you’re new to repeaters

  1. Program your radio with the output frequency, offset, and access tone.
  2. Listen for at least a minute to make sure the channel isn’t in use.
  3. Key up briefly, wait for the courtesy tone, then give your call sign.
  4. Want to chat? Just say your call and “monitoring” or “listening.”

If you’re unlicensed, please listen but don’t transmit — come to a study session and we’ll get you legal.

On-air etiquette

  • ID with your call sign at least every ten minutes and at the end of a QSO.
  • Leave a gap between transmissions so emergency traffic can break in.
  • Identify kerchunks — radio checks are fine, silent tests aren’t.
  • No commercial, political, or unkind content, please.
  • During a net, check in when the Net Control asks for your suffix.

Other repeaters worth programming

The Burbank area sits at the crossroads of several excellent repeater systems run by neighboring clubs. These are good places to hang out until BARC is on the air, and they’re worth having in memory any time you’re in town. Always verify current parameters on RepeaterBook before you key up.

Technical notes

For the radio-curious, here’s what we’re planning to install once coordination is finalized:

  • A commercial-grade UHF repeater at 25 W output, rack-mounted indoors.
  • A duplexer tuned to our pair, with a bandpass/band-reject configuration to coexist with nearby broadcast and land-mobile transmitters.
  • A single vertical antenna on a non-penetrating roof mount at roughly 160 m above ground level.
  • Controller with voice ID, courtesy tone, and a link-ready interface so we can experiment with IRLP/AllStar in the future.

Members with professional RF experience who’d like to help with the build, tuning, or site survey, please reach out.
